About Me

I didn’t start programming because I was “naturally gifted” or because everything made sense at the beginning. I started because I was curious, frustrated, and tired of only consuming technology instead of understanding how it works.

I’m a beginner Front-End Developer learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and most of what I know comes from building things, breaking them, and fixing them again. I’ve struggled with bugs that made no sense, designs that didn’t look right, and code that worked yesterday but not today. That process is exactly how I learn.

I build projects to grow, not to impress. Every website I create teaches me something new about structure, problem-solving, and patience. Web development is part of a bigger picture for me: I’m working toward a future in robotics engineering, where logic, systems, and persistence matter more than shortcuts. I’m still early in the journey, but I take it seriously. I show up, I keep learning, and I don’t quit when things get hard.
